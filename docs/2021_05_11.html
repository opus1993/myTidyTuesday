The #TidyTuesday data this week is on Internet Access, from Microsoft by way of The Verge.

If broadband access was a problem before 2020, the pandemic turned it into a crisis. As everyday businesses moved online, city council meetings or court proceedings became near-inaccessible to anyone whose connection couldn‚Äôt support a Zoom call. Some school districts started providing Wi-Fi hotspots to students without a reliable home connection. In other districts, kids set up in McDonald‚Äôs parking lots just to get a reliable enough signal to do their homework. After years of slowly widening, the broadband gap became impossible to ignore.

plot_il <- function(variable) {
  broadband_il %>%
    st_as_sf() %>%
